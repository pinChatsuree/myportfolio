zendesk re-design |

Zendesk is a support, sales, and customer engagement software that enable quick scalable in order to meet changes needed.

UI/UX > zendesk

Problem Statement

At first, I have to identify what are their current obstacles that prevent them to achieve their main goals. Their goals are to have a conversion rate more than 1.7%, gaining much well-known among targeted audiences, and those propect clients will become their long term customers.

What I had seen?

  • There are too many links on this page.
  • Three (3) CTAs can confuse users where they should take the action and finally miss achieving the goal.
  • Headline is too general.
  • They use their homepage as landing page which provided too many information on a particular purpose.

Market Research

The takeaways from good designs

Tons of landing page designs available on the market, from many departments, reveal that

  • Only one (1) CTA needed for a particular goal to achieve.
  • Never use homepage as landing page to avoid misleading users.
  • If further information needed, only to put few that concerned as most important or clients have to know in the below section.
  • Text and background have a high contrast to enrich the visibility.
    - Not too many links on the landing page.
  • Sections are clearly divided.

Generated from worse designs

Not to take only pros side, I have to consider bad design in order to avoid execute that onto this re-layouting.

  • Text heavy makes users leave the site faster.
  • Avoid using too complex visuals/images to escape from the messy.
  • Do not have a cleat Call to Action so users do not know what to do.
  • Design consistency should be implemented to it remembered.
  • Text and visual are blending and the headline is telling nothing.

High Fidelity Wireframe

Before move on to visual design, a wireframe was created in order to show the overall process of where this and that goes. Since this is only a landing page, so it needs no complexity but the clear CTA and related information.

Moderated User Testing

By this re-design of the landing page, found out that goal to get the Demo, had been
achieved more than 72% an with only few took longer time to finish.

User Interface Design

In the end, the colours are added to the artwork and from this the brand-colours are remained so that users remember what the brand is.

Moreover, colour of the CTA is set to a darker one which has a higher contrast between itself and surroundings. Headline is revised so it catchs more audiences and relevant information are at the bottom of the page.

At just slightly below 2% of conversion rate after the new design was launched comparing to 1.23% of that from the old version.
